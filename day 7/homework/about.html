HISTORY OF MERCEDES

Mercedes -Benz ( pronounced: [mɛʁˈtseːdəsˌbɛnts] ) is a German manufacturer of luxury passenger cars with particularly high demands and innovations , as well as trucks . The company was formerly officially called Daimler-Benz AG , and Mercedes was only the name of its cars. It was founded in its current form, under the name Mercedes-Benz, on June 28, 1926 , with the merger of the companies Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft and Benz & Cie . In Greece, Mercedes-Benz buses were manufactured by Viamax . In 1982 , Mercedes-Benz Hellas was founded , which is a 100% subsidiary of the corresponding German company. Daimler AG, as the parent company is now called , is the world's largest truck manufacturer and the second largest luxury car manufacturer. Mercedes-Benz's main competitors are BMW , Audi , Volvo , Lexus and DS Automobiles (the luxury subsidiary of Citroën ), while especially in North America , the Middle East and China they are also Cadillac and Lincoln .
